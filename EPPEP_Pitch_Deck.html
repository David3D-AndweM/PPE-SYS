The Problem

PPE compliance is broken
across mining operations

  • ⏰

    Expired PPE Goes Undetected

    Items expire with no automated alert. Workers enter hazardous zones without valid equipment. Safety officers only discover violations during manual audits.

  • 📋

    Paper-Based Issuing Is Slow & Inaccurate

    Paper picking slips get lost, damaged, or forged. Issuing takes 20+ minutes per employee. Stock records are manually updated — and frequently wrong.

  • 📦

    Stock-Outs Are Discovered Too Late

    Store officers only notice low stock when a worker arrives to collect. By then, the item may be out of stock and compliance is already broken.

  • 🔍

    Regulatory Audits Are Painful

    Preparing for a DMRE inspection means manually collating spreadsheets across departments and sites. The process takes 40+ hours and is error-prone.

  • 🔄

    Employee Transfers Break Compliance

    When an employee moves departments, nobody checks whether their PPE meets the new department's requirements. Gaps go undetected indefinitely.

PPE Issuing Workflow

The primary workflow. Covers expiry replacement, lost/damaged items, and new employee first-issue. Average time: 3–5 minutes end-to-end.

1
TRIGGER

PPE Need Identified

An item expires, is lost, damaged, or a new employee joins. The system can auto-detect expiry and pre-create a slip, or a Manager/Safety Officer can create one manually.

🏗️ Manager🦺 Safety Officer🤖 Auto (expiry engine)
2
CREATION

Picking Slip Created

A Picking Slip is generated — listing the employee, department, reason, and all PPE items needed. "Auto-Create" reads the department's PPE requirements and builds the list automatically. Slip enters PENDING status.

📋 Picking Slip Document📲 Notification sent to approvers
3
APPROVAL

Multi-Level Approval

The system checks the PPE configuration for this department and determines the required approval chain (e.g. Manager first, then Safety Officer). Each approver receives a push notification. Rejection at any stage captures a reason and notifies the requester.

🏗️ Manager approves🦺 Safety Officer approves
4
QR GENERATED

Slip Approved — QR Code Created

Once all approvals are complete, the slip status changes to APPROVED. A cryptographically signed QR code is generated and attached to the slip. The employee receives a "ready to collect" notification.

🔐 HMAC-SHA256 signed QR📲 Employee notified
5
SCAN

Employee Visits Store — QR Scanned

The employee presents the QR code (on their phone or printed). The Store Officer opens the scanner in the app and scans it. The system validates the signature, checks the slip is still APPROVED, and displays the items to issue.

⛏️ Employee📦 Store Officer scans
6
COMPLETE

Issue Confirmed — All Records Updated

Store Officer taps "Confirm Issue". In a single transaction: stock is deducted from the warehouse, a PPE Assignment is created for the employee with its expiry date, the slip moves to ISSUED, and the full audit trail is written.

📦 Stock deducted✅ PPE Assignment created📋 Audit trail logged

Expiry & Compliance Monitoring

Fully automated. No human triggers required. The system catches problems before the shift starts.

1
DAILY 00:30

Expiry Engine Runs

Every night at 00:30, a Celery scheduled task scans every active PPE Assignment across all employees and sites. Items past their expiry date are marked EXPIRED. Items within the configured grace period (default 7 days) are marked EXPIRING SOON.

🤖 Celery Beat (automated)
2
ALERTS

Pre-Expiry Alerts Dispatched

Employees receive a notification: "Your [SCSR] expires in 5 days — collect your replacement." Their manager receives the same alert. Critical PPE (SCSR, respirators) triggers escalation to the Safety Officer as well.

⛏️ Employee notified🏗️ Manager notified🦺 Safety Officer (critical PPE)
3
HOURLY

Stock Monitor Checks Reorder Levels

Every hour, the stock monitor compares current warehouse stock against each item's configured reorder level. If stock falls at or below the threshold (e.g. SCSR quantity drops to 3, reorder level is 5), an alert is sent to the Store Officer and Admin.

🤖 Celery (automated)📦 Store Officer alerted
4
LIVE

Manager Dashboard Updates in Real Time

As expiry statuses change, the Manager's team compliance dashboard updates via WebSocket — without the page refreshing. Compliance % drops live when an item expires; rises when replacement PPE is issued.

📊 WebSocket push🏗️ Manager sees live %

Key Technical Design Decisions Explained Simply

Why HMAC-Signed QR Codes?

A plain QR code is just a number — anyone can copy it or print a fake. Our QR codes contain a cryptographic signature generated from a secret key held only by the server. When the Store Officer scans, the server re-computes the signature and verifies it matches. A forged or tampered QR is rejected before any PPE is issued.

Why JSONB Approval Chains?

Approval requirements vary by PPE type and department. By storing the approval chain as structured data in the database, adding a new approval step (e.g. requiring a third sign-off for respiratory equipment) requires zero code changes — an admin updates a database record. The system adapts instantly.

Why Append-Only Audit Logs?

Regulatory compliance requires that historical records cannot be altered after the fact. Our audit log is designed to only ever add new entries — never update or delete. This provides a tamper-evident record that satisfies the most stringent mine safety inspections.

Security

Security built in,
not bolted on

🔐

Signed QR Codes (Anti-Forgery)

Every picking slip QR code is signed with HMAC-SHA256 using a server-held secret key. Any attempt to forge, copy, or replay a QR code is detected and rejected at scan time. Invalid scans are logged.

🛡️

Role-Based Access Control

Every API endpoint enforces role permissions. Employees cannot see other employees' data. Managers only see their department. Store Officers can only perform scanning and stock operations. Roles cannot be escalated by users.

📋

Immutable Audit Trail

Every state change is recorded permanently: who made it, when, what changed, and what the previous state was. The log cannot be modified — not even by administrators. Provides court-admissible evidence of compliance actions.

🔑

JWT with Embedded Claims

Access tokens carry the user's roles, site access list, and employee ID — eliminating per-request database queries for authorization. Tokens have short expiry windows. Refresh tokens rotate on use.

🔍

Scan Logging (Fraud Detection)

Every QR scan is recorded — including failed, mismatched, and expired attempts. A pattern of failed scans can indicate a forged document attempt. The full scan history is queryable by admin.

🏗️

Automated Security Scanning

Every code push triggers a Trivy security scan across the entire codebase. Results are uploaded to GitHub Security in SARIF format. Critical vulnerabilities block the CI pipeline from deploying.
